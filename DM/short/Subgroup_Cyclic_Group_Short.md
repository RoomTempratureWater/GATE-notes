# Subgroup & Cyclic Group - Short Notes

## 1. Cayley Table (Composition Table)
A grid that defines a finite group by showing all operation results.
- **Identity Element**: Row and column exactly match the header row and column.
- **Inverse Element**: Exists if the identity element appears exactly once in every row and column.

## 2. Subgroups
A subset $H$ of a group $G$ is a subgroup if $H \subseteq G$ and $H$ forms a group under $G$'s operation.
- **Union**: $H_1 \cup H_2$ is **NOT** necessarily a subgroup.
- **Intersection**: $H_1 \cap H_2$ is **ALWAYS** a subgroup.

### Lagrange's Theorem
For a finite group $G$ and its subgroup $H$:
**$|H|$ (order of $H$) divides $|G|$ (order of $G$)**.
*(Note: Reverse need not be true.)*

## 3. Cyclic Groups & Generators
- **Cyclic Group**: A group where every element can be generated by repeatedly applying the group operation on a single element. 
- **Generator**: The element that generates the entire group (e.g., $g^1, g^2, ...$ yields all elements).

### Number of Generators Formula (Euler's Totient $\phi(n)$)
If the cyclic group has order $n$, where $n = p_1^a \cdot p_2^b \cdot p_3^c \dots$ (prime factorization):
$$ \phi(n) = n \left( \frac{p_1 - 1}{p_1} \right) \left( \frac{p_2 - 1}{p_2} \right) \dots $$
